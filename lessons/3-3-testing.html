The "One Metric That Matters"

Don't track everything‚Äîfocus on one key metric that indicates if your core hypothesis is working. For an early MVP, this is usually:

Are users completing the core action that delivers value?

Collecting Feedback

Quantitative data (numbers) tells you WHAT is happening. Qualitative feedback (conversations) tells you WHY.

  • User interviews: 15-30 min calls with early users
  • Surveys: Quick feedback forms (keep them short!)
  • Session recordings: Watch how users actually use your product
  • Support messages: Every complaint is a learning opportunity

Interpret the Data

Your mentor matching MVP has been live for 2 weeks. Results: 50 students signed up, 30 were matched with mentors, but only 8 actually had a meeting. Feedback: "I got matched but didn't know what to do next."

What should you iterate on?

AGet more sign-ups through better marketing
BFind better mentors who are more responsive
CImprove the post-match experience with clear next steps

üåü Fix the Leak First!

The data shows a clear drop-off: 30 matches ‚Üí 8 meetings. The feedback explains why: unclear next steps. Add a clear email with "Here's your mentor, here's how to book a call" before worrying about more sign-ups.

üëç Maybe, But...

The problem might not be mentor quality‚Äîstudents said they didn't know what to do next. Fix the process before blaming people.

üí° Wrong Focus

More sign-ups won't help if 70% drop off before the core value (meeting). Fix the funnel leak before pouring more water in.

The Iteration Cycle

Friday: Launch the improvement
