Phase 4: Pitch & Launch

Launch Strategy

40 min read
Lesson 16 of 16

You've built your product, crafted your story, and prepared your pitch. Now it's time to launch. In this final lesson, you'll learn how to plan and execute a launch that generates buzz, attracts users, and creates momentum for your startup.

What is a Launch?

A launch is a coordinated effort to introduce your product to the world and attract your first wave of users.

Key Concept

A launch isn't a single moment‚Äîit's a campaign. The "launch day" is just the peak of a longer effort to build awareness, generate interest, and convert users.

A good launch helps you:

  • Get a concentrated burst of users for feedback
  • Build social proof quickly
  • Test your messaging and positioning
  • Generate press and word of mouth
  • Create momentum and motivation

Pre-Launch: Building Anticipation

The work starts weeks before launch day.

4+ Weeks Before Launch

Build a waitlist:

  • Create a landing page with email capture
  • Offer incentives for early signups (early access, discount, founding member status)
  • Use your networks to drive initial signups

Engage your community:

  • Share behind-the-scenes content
  • Ask for input on features
  • Build in public on social media

2 Weeks Before Launch

Prepare your assets:

  • Launch video or demo
  • Screenshots and product images
  • Written copy (tagline, description, features)
  • Social media posts pre-written

Line up support:

  • Ask friends and early supporters to share on launch day
  • Reach out to relevant influencers or communities
  • Prepare email to your waitlist

1 Week Before Launch

  • Test everything‚Äîmake sure the product works
  • Set up analytics to track launch metrics
  • Prepare for support requests
  • Schedule your launch posts
  • Send "coming soon" teaser to waitlist

Launch Platforms

Product Hunt

A community where people discover new products. Great for tech products.

Tips for Product Hunt:

  • Launch on Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday (most active days)
  • Launch at midnight PT (when the day resets)
  • Have friends upvote and comment (but don't ask directly‚Äîagainst rules)
  • Engage with every comment
  • Create a compelling tagline and description

Hacker News

A tech-focused community. Good for developer tools and tech products.

Tips for HN:

  • Use "Show HN:" prefix for your launch post
  • Be authentic and humble
  • Respond thoughtfully to all comments
  • Share your story, not just your product

Reddit

Find relevant subreddits for your target audience.

Tips for Reddit:

  • Be a genuine community member first
  • Follow subreddit rules carefully
  • Offer value, not just promotion
  • Engage with comments honestly

Social Media

Twitter/X, LinkedIn, TikTok, Instagram‚Äîchoose based on where your audience is.

Launch Day Execution

The Day Before

  • Final product check
  • Prep all posts and emails
  • Get good sleep!

Launch Day Timeline

Early morning:

  • Post to launch platforms (Product Hunt at midnight PT)
  • Send email to waitlist
  • Post on social media

Throughout the day:

  • Engage with every comment and question
  • Share updates on social media
  • Ask supporters to share
  • Monitor metrics in real-time

Evening:

  • Thank everyone who supported
  • Share results and highlights
  • Rest‚Äîyou'll need it for the days ahead

‚ö†Ô∏è Launch Day Reality Check

Things will go wrong. Servers might crash. There will be bugs. Your best-laid plans won't survive contact with reality. Stay calm, fix issues quickly, and keep engaging. How you handle problems is more important than having no problems.

Post-Launch: Maintaining Momentum

The launch is just the beginning. Here's how to keep the momentum going:

Week 1 After Launch

  • Respond to all feedback
  • Fix critical bugs immediately
  • Thank supporters publicly
  • Share metrics and learnings
  • Follow up with interested press/influencers

Weeks 2-4

  • Ship improvements based on feedback
  • Continue content marketing
  • Build on channels that worked
  • Start planning your next launch moment

Multiple Launches

One launch isn't enough. Plan to launch multiple times:

  • Initial MVP launch
  • Major feature launches
  • Milestone announcements (users, revenue)
  • Platform-specific launches (mobile app, new market)

Measuring Launch Success

What should you track?

  • Traffic: How many people visited your site?
  • Signups: How many people signed up?
  • Activation: How many completed the key action?
  • Conversion rate: What % of visitors converted?
  • Sources: Where did traffic come from?
  • Engagement: Comments, shares, mentions

What's a "Good" Launch?

It depends on your stage and goals. But generally:

  • Product Hunt: Top 5 for the day is great, any front page is good
  • Signups: 100+ on launch day is a solid start for most products
  • Press: Any coverage is a win early on

More important than numbers: Did you learn? Did you get feedback? Do you have momentum to build on?

The Launch Checklist

Use this checklist to prepare:

Product:

  • [ ] Core features work reliably
  • [ ] Onboarding flow is smooth
  • [ ] Analytics are set up
  • [ ] Can handle traffic spike

Marketing assets:

  • [ ] Landing page is compelling
  • [ ] Product images/screenshots ready
  • [ ] Demo video (if needed)
  • [ ] Social media posts drafted
  • [ ] Email to waitlist drafted

Launch platforms:

  • [ ] Platform accounts set up
  • [ ] Posts/listings prepared
  • [ ] Timing chosen

Support team:

  • [ ] Friends/supporters notified
  • [ ] Plan for engaging with comments
  • [ ] Support channels ready (email, chat)
